Building a Kossel Clear

written 2014-09-24

Over the summer I bought a set of parts to build a delta configuration Kossel 3D printer with an acrylic frame from Blue Eagle Labs. The kit uses over the shelf slides and bearings and the Ramps 1.4 shield for the Arduino Mega, to create a easily modifiable printer with an impressive print volume.

Motor Mounts

Each of the three corners on the base house a motor that manipulates the slides on the vertical rods. The corner mounts using the acrylic kit were made of ~8 pieces screwed together to from a single unit with the motor mounted in the center.

Each of the corners is connected using slotted aluminum channel to form the complete printer base.

Slides

The slide assemblies for the printer each consist of three bearings sandwiched between round acrylic pieces. The acrylic pieces have mounts for pivoting joints that allow the carriage arms to have a full range of motion.

The belts attach two two screws on the slide and have adjustable tensioners on the top of the frame

Extruder

The extruder assembly on the Kossel clear has a magnetic hinge for deploying an extra limit switch beneath the extruder. This allows the printer to auto-calibrate and level itself based on bed measurements.

After finishing the assembly, I had to disassemble the frame and load it in to a suitcase to take to school in Rochester. Surprisingly, nothing was damaged or lost in the process.
